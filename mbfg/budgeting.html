Budgeting for Military Life

Where Do I Start?

Managing finances in the military can be challenging due to frequent relocations, deployments, and transitioning to civilian life. A solid budget helps ensure financial stability and makes the most of the unique benefits available to service members. This guide will help you create a budget, maximize your military benefits, and plan for the future.

Understanding Your Military Pay & Benefits

Before building a budget, it's essential to understand all sources of income and benefits available to you.

Key Sources of Income:

  • Basic Pay - Your main salary, based on rank and years of service.
  • Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) - Helps cover housing costs (varies by location and dependency status).
  • Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS) - Helps cover food expenses for service members.
  • Special & Incentive Pays - Extra pay for specific duties (hazard pay, flight pay, sea pay, etc.).
  • Tax-Free Benefits - Many allowances (like BAH & BAS) are not taxable, reducing your tax burden.

Tip: Use the DFAS Military Pay Calculator to estimate your total earnings.

Creating a Military Budget

A good budget ensures you live within your means, save for the future, and prepare for unexpected expenses.

Steps to Create a Budget:

  1. Track Your Income & Expenses - List all sources of income (base pay, BAH, BAS, bonuses). Track monthly expenses (rent, utilities, food, insurance, car payments, debt, entertainment).
  2. Use the 50/30/20 Rule - 50% Needs: Housing, food, insurance, transportation, essential bills. 30% Wants: Dining out, shopping, entertainment, hobbies. 20% Savings & Debt Repayment: Emergency fund, TSP contributions, extra debt payments.

Saving for Emergencies & Deployment

Military life is unpredictable, so an emergency fund is crucial.

How Much Should You Save and Special Savings?

  • 3-6 Months of Expenses: Start with $1,000 and build up over time. Keep it in a separate, easy-to-access savings account.
  • Deployment Fund: Save extra for deployment expenses (childcare, pet care, travel, emergencies) of up to $10,000 during deployments. Combat Zone Tax Exclusion - Some or all of your earnings may be tax-free.
