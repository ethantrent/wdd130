Understanding Military Benefits

Types of Benefits

Navigating military benefits can feel overwhelming, but they are designed to support service members, veterans, and their families in areas like education, housing, healthcare, and financial security. This guide breaks down some of the most valuable benefits available, helping you understand what you qualify for and how to access them.

What is it?

The VA Home Loan Program helps eligible service members, veterans, and surviving spouses buy, build, or refinance a home without needing a down payment and with lower interest rates.

Key Benefits:

  • No Down Payment Required
  • No Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)
  • Competitive Interest Rates
  • Easier Qualification Compared to Conventional Loans

Who Qualifies?

  • Active-duty military, veterans, and National Guard/Reserve members (with required service time)
  • Surviving spouses of service members (in certain cases)

How to Apply: Visit VA.gov to apply and find a VA-approved lender.

What is it?

The GI Bill provides tuition assistance for veterans, service members, and their families, covering expenses for college, vocational training, and even some certification programs.

Types of GI Bill Programs:

  • Post-9/11 GI Bill - Covers full tuition & fees at public colleges, provides a monthly housing allowance, and pays for books & supplies.
  • Montgomery GI Bill (MGIB) - Provides financial assistance for college or vocational training.

Who Qualifies?

  • Active-duty military members and veterans with at least 90 days of service.
  • Eligible dependents through Transfer of Entitlement (TOE).

How to Use It: Apply through the VA's GI Bill website and check with your school's Veterans Affairs office.

What is it?

TRICARE is the military's healthcare program, providing comprehensive medical coverage for active-duty service members, retirees, and their families.

Key Plans:

  • TRICARE Prime - Best for active-duty service members & families (low costs, managed care).
  • TRICARE Select - Allows more flexibility in choosing providers (higher out-of-pocket costs).
  • TRICARE for Life - Coverage for military retirees eligible for Medicare.

More Info: Learn about plans and eligibility at TRICARE.mil

What Is It?

The Blended Retirement System (BRS) combines the traditional military pension with a 401(k)-style savings plan (TSP - Thrift Savings Plan).

Key Benefits:

  • Lifetime Pension (for those who serve 20+ years).
  • TSP Matching Contributions (military matches up to 5%).
  • Lump-Sum Retirement Options.
  • Learn More: Visit Military OneSource for retirement planning tools.

What is it?

The Servicemembers' Group Life Insurance (SGLI) provides affordable life insurance while serving, and Veterans' Group Life Insurance (VGLI) continues coverage after leaving the military.

Coverage Amounts:

  • SGLI: Up to $500,000 for active-duty personnel.
  • VGLI: Converts SGLI to post-service life insurance (must enroll within 1 year of leaving service).

Apply for SGLI or VGLI through VA Life Insurance.

  • Military Discounts - Many retailers, travel companies, and service providers offer special discounts (check our Military Discounts page).
  • Emergency Financial Assistance - Programs like the Army Emergency Relief (AER), Navy-Marine Corps Relief Society (NMCRS), and Air Force Aid Society (AFAS) provide help during financial hardships.
  • Transition Assistance Program (TAP) - Helps service members shift to civilian life with job training, career counseling, and financial planning.
